# Document Types

Use these structures as starting points. Adapt sections to the user's requested format, project context, and delivery approach.

## Project Charter

Include:

- project title
- project purpose
- business need or opportunity
- desired outcomes and measurable objectives
- success criteria
- high-level scope
- major deliverables
- assumptions and constraints
- high-level timeline or milestones
- high-level budget or funding note, if known
- key stakeholders
- high-level risks
- governance and approval approach
- sponsor approval section

## Project Management Plan

Include:

- project overview
- delivery approach: predictive, adaptive, or hybrid
- scope management
- schedule management
- cost management
- quality management
- resource management
- communications management
- risk management
- procurement management, if relevant
- stakeholder engagement
- change control
- governance and decision rights
- measurement and reporting
- assumptions, constraints, and open questions

## Scope Statement

Include:

- product scope description
- project scope description
- business objectives supported
- in-scope capabilities
- out-of-scope capabilities
- MVP boundaries
- deliverables
- acceptance criteria for scope completion
- assumptions and constraints
- exclusions
- dependencies
- approval section

## Work Breakdown Structure

Include:

- WBS ID
- work package name
- description
- related requirement, feature, epic, or deliverable
- owner or responsible role
- estimated complexity or effort range
- dependencies
- acceptance or completion criteria
- notes and assumptions

## Delivery Roadmap

Include:

- delivery phase or increment
- objective
- major capabilities
- key deliverables
- dependencies
- decision points
- risks
- acceptance or readiness criteria
- estimated timing or sequencing

## Milestone Schedule

Include:

- milestone
- milestone description
- target timeframe
- entry criteria
- exit criteria
- dependent decisions
- owner
- key risks or assumptions

## Stakeholder Register

Include:

- stakeholder name or group
- role or relationship to project
- interest
- influence
- expectations
- current engagement
- desired engagement
- communication needs
- engagement approach
- owner

## Risk Register

Include:

- risk ID
- risk statement
- cause
- potential impact
- probability rating
- impact rating
- priority
- response strategy
- response actions
- owner
- trigger
- status

## Assumptions and Open Questions Log

Include:

- item ID
- assumption or question
- category
- impact if incorrect or unresolved
- owner
- needed-by date or decision point
- current status
- resolution notes

## Decision Log

Include:

- decision ID
- decision summary
- context
- options considered
- decision owner
- decision date
- rationale
- impacted scope, schedule, cost, risk, or stakeholder group
- follow-up actions

## Issue Log

Include:

- issue ID
- issue description
- impact
- priority
- owner
- target resolution date
- current status
- decision or action needed

## Communications Plan

Include:

- stakeholder or audience
- information need
- communication method
- frequency
- sender or owner
- format or channel
- escalation path
- feedback mechanism

## Change Control Plan

Include:

- scope baseline and change control purpose
- change request intake process
- impact assessment criteria
- decision authority
- approval workflow
- emergency change handling
- communication process
- traceability and versioning rules

## Change Request

Include:

- change request ID
- requester
- date
- change summary
- rationale
- affected scope, schedule, cost, quality, risk, and stakeholders
- options considered
- recommendation
- approval decision
- implementation notes

## Benefits Realization Plan

Include:

- expected benefit
- strategic objective supported
- benefit owner
- measurement method
- baseline
- target
- realization timeframe
- dependencies
- risks to realization
- reporting cadence

## Governance Plan

Include:

- governance objectives
- roles and decision rights
- steering cadence
- escalation paths
- change authority
- risk and issue escalation rules
- reporting expectations
- audit or compliance needs

## Status Report

Include:

- reporting period
- overall health
- accomplishments
- upcoming work
- schedule status
- budget status
- scope status
- risks and issues
- decisions needed
- change requests
- support needed

## Lessons Learned Report

Include:

- project context
- what worked well
- what did not work well
- root causes
- recommendations
- actions to carry forward
- owners for follow-up improvements

## Project Closeout Report

Include:

- project summary
- final scope and deliverables
- acceptance status
- schedule performance
- budget performance
- benefits outlook
- unresolved risks or issues
- operational handoff
- lessons learned summary
- closure approval

## Requirements Management Plan

Include:

- purpose and scope of requirements management
- requirements sources and stakeholder groups
- elicitation methods
- analysis and prioritization approach
- requirements categories and taxonomy
- documentation standards and templates
- traceability approach
- validation and acceptance process
- change control process
- versioning and baseline rules
- roles and responsibilities
- tools and repositories
- quality criteria for requirements
- reporting and governance cadence

## Product Requirements Document

Include:

- product or feature overview
- business problem or opportunity
- target users and stakeholder needs
- goals and measurable outcomes
- in-scope and out-of-scope items
- assumptions and constraints
- functional requirements
- non-functional requirements
- user experience requirements
- data requirements
- integration requirements
- reporting or analytics requirements
- compliance, security, privacy, or operational requirements
- dependencies
- risks and open questions
- acceptance and release criteria

## Requirements Traceability Matrix

Include:

- requirement ID
- requirement statement
- source or stakeholder
- business objective or outcome
- priority
- requirement type
- related user story, feature, epic, or deliverable
- design or architecture reference
- implementation reference
- test case or validation method
- acceptance status
- change history or version
- owner

## User Stories and Acceptance Criteria

Include:

- epic or feature grouping
- story ID
- user role or persona
- user need
- business value
- user story statement
- acceptance criteria
- priority
- dependencies
- assumptions
- notes for design, engineering, QA, or operations

Use acceptance criteria that are testable and unambiguous. Prefer the Given/When/Then format when behavior, state, or workflow matters.

## Specification by Example / BDD Scenarios

Include:

- feature name
- business rule or requirement covered
- scenario title
- preconditions
- Given steps
- When action
- Then expected result
- examples table, if inputs and outputs vary
- edge cases
- negative or exception scenarios
- traceability to requirement, story, or test case

Keep scenarios executable in spirit even if they are delivered as documentation.

## API Requirements and Integration Contract

Include:

- integration overview
- consumer and provider systems
- business capability supported
- endpoint or interface list
- request methods and paths
- authentication and authorization requirements
- request payloads
- response payloads
- data definitions and validation rules
- error codes and failure behavior
- idempotency, retries, and timeout expectations
- rate limits and performance requirements
- security, privacy, and compliance requirements
- versioning and backward compatibility rules
- logging, monitoring, and audit expectations
- test, sandbox, and production environment notes
- open questions and assumptions

## Requirements Documentation Index

Include:

- document or artifact name
- artifact purpose
- owning role or team
- status
- version
- location or repository path
- related project phase or delivery increment
- related requirements, epics, features, or systems
- approval status
- next review date
- notes or gaps

## Data Requirements and Data Dictionary

Include:

- data entity or object
- business meaning
- key attributes
- data type or format
- required or optional status
- validation rules
- source system
- owner
- privacy, retention, or compliance notes
- related requirements, screens, APIs, reports, or events

## Non-Functional Requirements

Include:

- requirement ID
- category: performance, security, privacy, reliability, usability, accessibility, scalability, maintainability, supportability, compliance, observability, or recovery
- requirement statement
- rationale
- measurement method
- acceptance threshold
- priority
- owner
- dependencies or assumptions

## QA Preparation Plan

Include:

- test strategy
- requirement-to-test traceability approach
- test levels and types
- acceptance criteria coverage
- BDD or SBE scenario coverage
- test data needs
- environment needs
- defect workflow
- roles and responsibilities
- entry and exit criteria
- risks and dependencies

## Engineering Handoff Package

Include:

- product and delivery context
- scope summary
- MVP boundaries
- prioritized requirements
- user stories and acceptance criteria
- BDD or SBE scenarios
- API and integration requirements
- data requirements
- non-functional requirements
- technology stack assumptions
- dependencies
- risks
- open questions
- implementation planning notes

## Release Readiness Checklist

Include:

- release scope
- requirements completion status
- test completion status
- defect status
- security and privacy readiness
- operational readiness
- support readiness
- deployment readiness
- rollback plan status
- stakeholder approval status
- go or no-go decision
